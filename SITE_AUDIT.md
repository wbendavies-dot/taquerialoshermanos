# Taqueria Los Hermanos — Current Website Audit

**Site:** [taquerialoshermanos.com](https://www.taquerialoshermanos.com/)
**Audited:** June 10, 2026
**Platform:** Wix
**Business:** 5 locations in metro Atlanta (Tucker, Lilburn, Suwanee, Lawrenceville, Dunwoody), family-owned since 2001 by three brothers — Miguel, Roel, and Raul. Online ordering via Toast (per-location links). Catering offered with published per-person pricing.

---

## What's already working (preserve these)

Before the criticism — the site has real assets most local restaurant sites lack:

- **Published catering pricing** ($12.95–$16.95/person, 5 buffet menus, itemized desserts). Price transparency pre-qualifies leads — most competitors hide pricing. Keep this.
- **Toast online ordering** with one-click, per-location links. Toast is the restaurant's own ordering channel (flat-fee, not a 15–30% marketplace commission like DoorDash) — the platform choice is fine; the integration is the problem.
- **A genuine story**: three named brothers, 25 years in business, "100% fresh meat," southwestern Mexican coast recipes. The raw material for the brand moat exists — it's just buried in one paragraph.
- **Some testimonials** (3) and active Facebook/Instagram.
- **Digital gift cards** (buried on the ordering page).

---

## 1. Weaknesses

### The menu fails its job — and the menu is 80% of why people visit
- **No prices anywhere.** The text menu shows none; the gallery page says "(PRICES VARY BY LOCATION)" and points back to the menu page — which also has no prices. This is a dead loop on the site's most-visited content. Research: 80% of visitors come specifically for the menu; 75% expect a browsable menu.
- **Menu is split across two confusing pages**: `/menu` (text-only, no photos, no prices) and `/menu-1` (photo gallery with no dish names or prices). Neither page works alone; together they still don't answer "what does it cost and what does it look like?"
- **No photos attached to items, no dietary labels, no search/filter.** Photo menus convert 25% more; 84% of guests look at photos before ordering.
- **Menu is not connected to ordering.** A visitor reads the menu, then must find "Order Online" in the nav, pick a location, and re-find the dish inside Toast. Every extra step costs orders.

### Catering form leaks the highest-value leads
- Form fields are **name / email / subject / message** — a generic contact form. No event date, guest count, event type, or location. The restaurant learns nothing, and the lead does the work.
- **"We respond within at least 72 hours."** The research benchmark is one business hour — speed-to-lead is the #1 catering conversion factor. A 72-hour promise tells a corporate planner to keep shopping. It also contradicts the "48-hour notice" order requirement on the same page.
- **Catering contact is a Gmail address** (Taquerialoshermanoscatering@gmail.com) and two personal cell numbers. Functional, but weak for corporate buyers.
- **No event photos, no catering testimonials, no policies** (delivery fees, setup, deposits, cancellation, dietary accommodations).

### Brand and platform hygiene
- The catering page lives at **`/copy-of-menu`** — a leftover Wix duplicate-page slug, visible in every shared link and search result.
- Homepage title tag is **"Mexican Food | Taqueria Los Hermanos | United States"** — the Wix location placeholder was never set. "United States" is the only geographic signal on the most important SEO element of the site.
- Hero tagline ("your taste buds in flavor town") undercuts a 25-year authentic family brand.
- Navigation overflow ("More...") hides pages.

## 2. Missed Opportunities

1. **The family story is the moat and it's one paragraph.** Three brothers, 25 years, coastal recipes — this is exactly what Tacombi built a brand on. No story page, no photos of the brothers, no timeline, no kitchen footage.
2. **5 locations = 5 local-SEO surfaces, currently 0 used.** One generic site serves all five cities. No per-location pages means no ranking for "Mexican restaurant Tucker GA," "tacos Lilburn," etc. — five separate markets' worth of searches conceded to competitors.
3. **Zero visitor capture.** No email/SMS signup, no first-order incentive, no loyalty mention. Every visitor who doesn't order today is lost permanently. (Comp: Talkin' Tacos collected 40,000 contacts and built $120K/month direct sales on this mechanic.)
4. **Catering pricing transparency — their rare advantage — isn't merchandised.** It's a wall of text. With photos, packaging ("Taco Bar for 20 = $299"), and an instant-quote feel, it would be a lead machine.
5. **Gift cards exist but are buried** on the ordering page instead of promoted (holidays, corporate gifting pairs naturally with catering).
6. **No specials/events content** — no Taco Tuesday, happy hour, or seasonal items to give regulars a reason to revisit the site or join a list.

## 3. Outdated Design Patterns

- **Text-wall menu with no prices** — pre-2015 pattern; functions as a phone-order reference card.
- **Separate "photo gallery" page** disconnected from the menu — galleries replaced by photographed menu items years ago.
- **Generic contact form** as the catering funnel.
- **Default Wix artifacts** (`/copy-of-menu`, "United States" title, "More..." nav) signal template, not brand.
- **Static hero with decorative tagline** instead of a conversion hero (CTA stack + hours/location at a glance).
- **Ordering as an exit** — clicking "Order Online" dumps users onto toasttab.com pages with two inconsistent URL formats, leaving the brand experience entirely.

## 4. Missing Conversion Opportunities

| Gap | Fix | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| No prices on menu | Priced HTML menu (per-location selector if prices differ) | 80% visit for the menu; price-less menus can't convert |
| Menu items not orderable | Deep-link each item/category to the right Toast location menu | PDF→HTML+ordering shift = up to +58% completed orders |
| No sticky mobile "Order Now" | Sticky bottom bar on mobile with Order / Call / Directions | Optimized CTA placement +34% conversion |
| Hero has no CTA stack | "Order Online · View Menu · Catering" above the fold | CTA above fold within 2 seconds |
| No email capture / incentive | Pop-up or inline signup with first-order offer | Direct-database restaurants grow 5–10x faster |
| Catering form too generic | Multi-step form (event type → date/guests → contact) + instant auto-reply + 1-hour response target | Speed-to-lead is the #1 catering conversion factor |
| No upsell path | Toast handles checkout upsells — configure popular pairings | +15–25% AOV |

## 5. Missing Trust Signals

- **No live Google ratings** on site (only 3 static testimonials). 74% of guests check reviews/offers on restaurant sites. Five locations likely have thousands of Google reviews — none surfaced.
- **No catering social proof**: no event photos, no client names/types (offices, schools, weddings, quinceañeras), no catering-specific reviews.
- **Gmail + personal cells** as the catering identity instead of catering@taquerialoshermanos.com.
- **No policies published** for catering (deposits, delivery, setup, cancellations) — uncertainty kills high-value bookings.
- **"100% Fresh Meat" claim** stated once, never substantiated with kitchen/sourcing story or photos.
- **No press, awards, or "since 2001" badge** treatment despite 25 years of operating history — longevity is a trust signal competitors can't fake.

## 6. Missing Local SEO Opportunities

- **Title tags**: "…| United States" instead of "…| Tucker, Lilburn, Suwanee, Lawrenceville & Dunwoody GA" (or per-page city titles). The single highest-leverage 10-minute fix on the site.
- **No per-location landing pages.** Each of the 5 locations needs its own URL with address, hours, phone, embedded map, photos, location Toast link, and city keywords. This is the difference between ranking in 1 map-pack and 5.
- **No structured data**: Restaurant/LocalBusiness schema, Menu schema, per-location NAP markup — all absent (Wix defaults don't cover multi-location).
- **URL slugs** like `/copy-of-menu` and `/menu-1` waste keyword opportunities (`/catering`, `/menu`).
- **No Google Business Profile ↔ site linkage strategy**: GBP menu links should point at the priced HTML menu; GBP "catering" service category should link the catering page; ordering links should use Toast deep links.
- **Menu content invisible to search**: dish names exist as text (good) but with no prices/schema, the site can't win "carne asada tacos near me"-style queries or show menu rich results.
- **No city-specific content** anywhere on the site — the words "Atlanta," "Tucker," "Lilburn" etc. barely appear in indexable copy.

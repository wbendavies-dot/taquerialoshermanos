# Los Hermanos Website Redesign — Research Report

**Date:** June 10, 2026
**Goal:** Identify what actually drives online orders, catering inquiries, and customer engagement on restaurant websites, so the redesign is built around revenue — not just aesthetics.

---

## Executive Summary

The research converges on one conclusion: **a restaurant website is an ordering machine first and a brand experience second.** The data is unambiguous:

- **91% of guests visit the website before ordering** takeout/delivery, and **80% come primarily to see the menu** (Owner.com survey of 1,300 US guests).
- **83% of restaurant searches happen on mobile**, and **89% of mobile restaurant searches result in a call or visit within 24 hours**.
- **69% of guests abandon confusing or poorly designed sites**; restaurants with mobile-optimized sites capture **42% more online orders**.
- **67% of consumers prefer ordering directly from a restaurant's own site** — yet third-party apps take **15–30% commission** on every order they capture instead.
- Switching from a PDF menu to a searchable HTML menu drives up to **58% more completed orders**.

For Los Hermanos, the highest-leverage moves are: a fast mobile-first site, an HTML menu with photography, frictionless first-party online ordering, and a dedicated catering funnel with a fast-response inquiry system.

## 2. What the Best Mexican Restaurant Websites Do

Sources: [Wiksit's Mexican template analysis](https://wiksit.com/blog/best-mexican-restaurant-website-templates), [HTMLBurger's standout designs](https://htmlburger.com/blog/restaurant-website-design/), plus live sites ([Tacombi](https://www.tacombi.com/menu), [Torchy's Tacos](https://torchystacos.com/), [Bartaco](https://order.bartaco.com/)).

### Visual language that works for Mexican restaurants
- **Warm, earthy palettes** — terra cotta, gold, deep red — signal authenticity instantly without resorting to clichés (sombreros, cartoon cacti).
- **Bold typography + hand-crafted/illustrated accents** convey family tradition and handmade food (e.g., hand-painted branding, illustrated ingredients).
- **Story-forward content**: Tacombi leads with Yucatán origins and taqueros preserving culinary tradition. Heritage is a differentiator chains can't copy — Los Hermanos ("The Brothers") has a family story built into its name.

### Structural patterns from leading Mexican brands
- **Tacombi** runs three distinct funnels: main brand site (story + menu), `order.tacombi.com` (ordering), and `catering.tacombi.com` (a *dedicated catering subdomain* — they treat catering as its own business line).
- **Torchy's** puts "Order Online" and its app front and center with a skip-the-line value proposition; brand voice ("Damn Good") is consistent everywhere.
- **Suerte (Austin)** pairs bright, energetic design with easy menu exploration — upscale without being stiff.
- Fast-casual layouts (e.g., the "Burrito Bar" pattern) put **large, tappable menu category blocks** front and center for delivery-first operations.

**Takeaway:** the best Mexican restaurant sites balance *cultural warmth + family story* with *chain-grade ordering UX*. Most local Mexican restaurants do neither well — this is the opportunity.

## 3. Restaurant UX Trends — 2026

Sources: [DoorDash's 2026 restaurant website guide](https://merchants.doordash.com/en-us/blog/building-restaurant-website), [Chowly's conversion-focused design examples](https://chowly.com/resources/blogs/restaurant-website-design-real-examples-that-convert-visitors-into-orders/), [Paytronix's 17 UX principles](https://www.paytronix.com/blog/ux-principles), [Flipdish's online ordering guide](https://www.flipdish.com/us/resources/blog/restaurant-online-ordering-guide), [BeyondMenu](https://get.beyondmenu.com/blog/increase-online-orders-restaurants/), [Unicorn Platform's conversion-page system](https://unicornplatform.com/blog/food-delivery-conversion-pages-in-2026/).

### Trends with measurable impact (prioritize these)
1. **First-party ordering** — eliminates 15–30% third-party commission; 67% of consumers prefer ordering direct.
2. **Mobile-optimized "Order Now" CTAs** (size + placement) — **+34% conversion**.
3. **Video-first hero sections** — **+35% time on site** (sizzling fajitas, tortillas being pressed by hand).
4. **Searchable HTML menus** replacing PDFs — up to **+58% completed orders**.
5. **Professional food photography** — 45% of visitors look for food photos first; **+30–40% conversion** on reservations/orders; photo menus convert **25% more** than text-only.
6. **AI-powered menu personalization / smart upsells** — **+15–25% average order value**.
7. **Micro-animations on scroll** and immediate interactive feedback — perceived quality and clarity.
8. **Accessibility-first (WCAG 2.1)** — legal protection + larger addressable audience.
9. **Voice search & local SEO optimization** — 27% of searches are voice-based; 66% of guests find restaurants via Google.
10. **Trust transparency** — show full pricing including fees upfront (Paytronix: hidden fees are a top abandonment driver).

### Core UX principles for the ordering flow (Paytronix)
- Fewest possible steps to checkout; guest checkout always available
- Progressive disclosure in menus (categories → items → modifiers)
- Visual hierarchy: one primary action per screen
- Easy cart editing; immediate error flagging; persistent visible cart/CTA while scrolling

### Retention data
- **87% of guests are more likely to reorder** from a restaurant with an excellent online experience
- Restaurants with direct customer databases grow **5–10x faster** than third-party-dependent ones ([Evokad first-party data guide](https://evokad.com/restaurant-first-party-data-guide-2026/))
- Restaurants with apps/loyalty see **85% more repeat customers**

## 4. Mobile-First Design — Best Practices & Statistics

Sources: [Owner.com's survey data](https://www.owner.com/blog/restaurant-website-design), [Chowly's 7 elements](https://chowly.com/resources/blogs/restaurant-website-design-7-elements-of-a-high-converting-restaurant-website/), [ChowNow best practices](https://get.chownow.com/blog/restaurant-website-best-practices-and-examples/), [BentoBox's 10 essential elements](https://www.getbento.com/blog/the-10-essential-elements-of-a-restaurant-website/), [Designodin](https://blog.designodin.com/mobile-first-why-it-matters-more-for-restaurants/).

### The numbers
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Restaurant searches on mobile | **83%** |
| Guests reading menus on phones before visiting | **72%** |
| Mobile searches → call/visit within 24 hrs | **89%** |
| Extra orders captured by mobile-optimized sites | **+42%** |
| Order abandonment per extra second of load time | **+7%** |
| Mobile users more likely to abandon non-optimized sites | **5x** |
| Target load time on mobile connections | **< 2 seconds** |

### Best practices
- **Thumb-zone design**: primary CTAs in the lower two-thirds of the screen; sticky bottom "Order Now" bar on mobile.
- **No zooming, ever**: menus readable at default size; tap targets ≥ 44px.
- **One-tap essentials**: call, directions, hours, order — reachable from anywhere on the site.
- **Performance budget**: compressed/next-gen images (WebP/AVIF), lazy loading below the fold, minimal JS. Every second costs 7% of orders.
- **Click-to-call and map links** must be native (`tel:`, geo links) — 89% of mobile searchers act within a day.

## 5. Catering Lead Generation — Best Practices

Sources: [Lavu's catering lead system guide](https://lavu.com/how-to-build-a-restaurant-catering-lead-generation-system/), [CaterZen on inquiry forms](https://www.caterzen.com/catering-inquiry-web-forms), [Podium](https://www.podium.com/article/generate-leads-for-catering-businesses), [WebFX catering lead gen](https://www.webfx.com/industries/local-consumer-services/catering/lead-generation/), [Birdeye](https://birdeye.com/blog/proven-tactics-for-restaurant-lead-generation-success/).

Catering is the highest-AOV channel a restaurant has (typical orders $300–$2,000+), and most local restaurants bury it under a generic contact form. The system that converts:

1. **Dedicated catering page (or subdomain, like Tacombi)** — not a paragraph on the About page. Own URL for ads and SEO ("Mexican catering [city]").
2. **Real event photography** — actual spreads, taco bars, office drop-offs. Not stock.
3. **HTML catering menu with per-person pricing and dietary filters** — price transparency qualifies leads before they ever call.
4. **Multi-step inquiry form** (event type → date/guest count → contact info). Multi-step forms convert better than long single forms because the first step is low-commitment. CTA must be **above the fold, visible within 2 seconds**.
5. **Instant auto-confirmation + fast human follow-up** — respond **within one business hour**; speed-to-lead is the single biggest conversion factor.
6. **Automated email nurture** — welcome → menu highlights → testimonials, for leads that don't book immediately.
7. **CRM (or even a spreadsheet) for every lead** — track cost per lead, inquiry→order conversion rate, and average catering order value. Lavu's example: a $50 lead at 10% conversion on a $400 order = $500 cost per order — tracking is what catches this.
8. **Social proof specific to catering** — testimonials from offices, weddings, quinceañeras; named local businesses if possible.
9. **Local SEO**: Google Business Profile with catering service listed, location-keyword landing copy.

## Strategic Conclusions for Los Hermanos

1. **The menu IS the website.** 80% of visitors come for it. Make it a beautiful, fast, photographed, searchable HTML menu — and make every item orderable from the menu itself.
2. **Mobile is the site.** Design every screen for a phone held in one hand at 6pm by a hungry person; desktop is the adaptation.
3. **First-party ordering is the profit story.** Every order moved from DoorDash to the site recovers 15–30% margin. The site's job is to make direct ordering *easier* than the apps.
4. **Catering deserves its own funnel.** Highest order values, least competition from third-party apps, and almost no local competitor does it well. A dedicated page + fast-response inquiry system is found money.
5. **Family story is the moat.** "Los Hermanos" has built-in heritage. Warm authentic visual identity + real family narrative is what Chipotle and the apps can't replicate — and it's what converts a first-time visitor into a regular.
6. **Capture every visitor.** Email/SMS capture with a first-order incentive turns one-time traffic into a re-marketable database — the channel where restaurants grow 5–10x faster.
